Asbestos was once a staple in British construction due to its fire‑resistant and insulating properties. Unfortunately, this means many properties across Hammersmith & Fulham still harbour asbestos in roofing materials, floor tiles, pipe lagging and textured coatings. Even seemingly minor renovation works can disturb fibres, so it’s essential to engage qualified specialists. Our surveys reveal hidden sources of asbestos and help clients plan refurbishments with confidence, preventing costly delays and health risks. We educate property owners about the three main types of asbestos commonly encountered — chrysotile, amosite and crocidolite — and the health implications of each. By understanding how and where these materials were used prior to their UK ban in 1999, we can anticipate their presence and prepare appropriate control measures. Long‑term exposure to asbestos fibres can cause serious illnesses such as mesothelioma, asbestosis and lung cancer; prompt identification and removal is the best way to protect future generations.

At Envirosafe Removal Specialists we carry out a comprehensive suite of services: management, refurbishment and demolition surveys; sample testing; licensed removal of sprayed coatings, insulation boards and pipe lagging; non‑licensed removal of cement sheeting, vinyl tiles and bitumen products; and encapsulation or repair when removal isn’t the best option. Our versatile team can assist with residential projects, large commercial refurbishments, public sector works and industrial contracts. We tailor the approach to each building’s construction and the client’s budget and timescale. Beyond physical removal, we can prepare asbestos management plans, update building registers and provide ongoing monitoring to ensure compliance. Because no two properties are the same, we believe in bespoke solutions that deliver value without compromising on safety. We liaise with regulatory authorities to secure any necessary notifications and provide consignment notes for waste shipments, ensuring the administrative side of your project runs smoothly.

Every project begins with a rigorous risk assessment. We isolate the work area using negative‑pressure enclosures and HEPA filtration, establish decontamination units and wear appropriate respiratory protection. Once the site is controlled, we carefully remove the asbestos‑containing materials using techniques that minimise fibre release, then double‑bag and seal waste for transport to licensed disposal facilities. Air monitoring and clearance testing provide reassurance that the area is safe for reoccupation. We document every step, giving clients peace of mind and a clear audit trail. Clear communication is vital: before we begin, we provide method statements outlining the sequence of works and expected timescales. During the project, a dedicated supervisor is on hand to answer questions and keep you informed of progress. After removal, we carry out a meticulous clean‑up using vacuum systems and surface sealing to capture any residual dust and prevent future fibre release.
